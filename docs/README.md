# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management Docs. This README provides an overview of the processes by which OctoAcme runs and delivers projects, along with convenient links to all process documentation.

## Project Management Process Summary

OctoAcme's project management processes are built around clear structure, stakeholder alignment, and iterative delivery. Projects begin with a lightweight yet thorough initiation phase, ensuring that each new initiative has a well-articulated business need, defined success metrics, and confirmed stakeholder engagement. The team documents a "Project One-pager" outlining objectives, stakeholders, timelines, and risks, supported by a high-level planning checklist and decision gate before moving to detailed planning. This disciplined approach ensures early clarity and prevents misalignment as teams progress through the project lifecycle.

Central to OctoAcme's execution is its emphasis on defined roles and regular collaboration. Core personas include Project Managers, who coordinate schedules and risks; Product Managers, who own vision and prioritization; Developers, who deliver and test features; and QA/Testing experts. Communication is structured through daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and monthly stakeholder updates, ensuring timely escalation and resolution of blockers. Project artifacts such as charters, risk registers, release plans, and retrospectives are maintained as living documents, enabling transparency and accountability.

Workflows hinge on version-controlled project boards, small and well-structured pull requests, and automation via CI pipelines for testing and linting. Acceptance criteria, definitions of done, and risk registers are embedded throughout the planning and execution processes to promote measurable outcomes. Quality assurance is rigorously maintained through mandatory unit tests, integration and end-to-end smoke tests, security scanning, and manual QA where required. Releases follow standardized checklists—covering backup, deployment, verification, and rollback—to minimize operational risk and maximize observability.

Continuous improvement is embedded through retrospectives after each significant milestone or incident, where action items are added to the backlog and success is measured. This feedback-rich process, along with clear escalation paths and regular review of improvement initiatives, fosters a culture of learning and adaptability within OctoAcme's teams. Overall, the OctoAcme project management system is designed for clarity, repeatability, and consistent delivery of high-quality software products through well-defined roles, strong communication habits, and a robust focus on quality and improvement.
